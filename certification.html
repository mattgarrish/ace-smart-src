Certification

The DAISY Consortium certifies organizations who have demonstrated that they have the knowledge, processes and procedures in place to check EPUB publications against the accessibility requirements of the EPUB Accessibility specification.

In order to become certified, applicants are required to provide detailed information on how their processes and workflows ensure that all aspects of conformance are covered. As every workflow is unique, the DAISY Consortium does not define specific testing requirements. This tool has been developed to highlight the key areas of the specification, and all applicants will need to show how they cover each area. A publisher might include quality checks within their publishing workflow, for example, and only spot check content at the end, while a third-party evaluator would need to demonstrate the thoroughness of their quality assurance testing on the final products they receive.

All applicants will be required to provide samples of work they have evaluated and their reporting practices. In the case of organizations, training and evaluation procedures will also be required to ensure that staff turnover does not affect their ability to carry out proper evaluations.

The DAISY Consortium does not warrant the evaluations or services provided by any certified individual or organization.
